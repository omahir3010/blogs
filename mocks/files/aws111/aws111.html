A Simple Guide to Uploading Files to Amazon S3

Step 1: Sign in to AWS

Begin by navigating to the AWS Management Console and logging in with your AWS account credentials.

Step 2: Access Amazon S3

Once in the AWS Console, locate and click on "S3" under the "Services" section.

Step 3: Create a Storage Bucket (if needed)

If you don't have a bucket yet, click "Create bucket." Assign it a unique name, choose a region, and follow the prompts to complete the creation process.

Specify a unique and S3-formatted name for the bucket

Enabling blocking ensures that the bucket will not be publicly accessible

Click on Create Bucket to create a bucket

Step 4: Open Your Storage Bucket

Click on the name of the bucket you created or an existing one to open its details.

Step 5: Upload Your File

Within the bucket, click "Upload." Add your desired file(s), proceed to the next steps, and set permissions (usually, keeping it private). Confirm and complete the upload process.

Step 6: Verify the Upload

Upon successful upload, you'll receive a confirmation message. Click on the uploaded file within your S3 bucket to confirm its presence.

Note: Congratulations! You've accomplished a seamless file upload to Amazon S3 using the AWS Console. If you have additional files to upload, follow these steps as needed. This method is perfect for occasional use or when dealing with a small number of files. For frequent or bulk file transfers, consider exploring other tools such as AWS CLI or SDKs.
